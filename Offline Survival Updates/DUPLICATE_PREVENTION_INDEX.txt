Merge rules for future work
1. If a new entry only repeats high-level advice already present in another file, expand the stronger existing entry instead of adding a new one.
2. If a new entry differs only by wording but not practical outcome, merge it.
3. If a new entry is a regional or advanced special case, link it to the broader existing topic rather than duplicating the base guidance.
4. Keep water, sanitation, and disease content tightly cross-referenced to avoid repeated hygiene text.
5. Keep psychology, leadership, and household routine content distinct:
   - panic = immediate stabilization
   - leadership = group order and fairness
   - routine = daily continuity
6. Keep dystopian / surveillance / AI-resilience content distinct:
   - digital dependency = paper/manual fallback
   - automation failure = human-operated continuity
7. Keep history entries lesson-focused; do not duplicate story background without extracting practical lessons.

Known overlap zones already handled
- Water safety is split between purification and rationing/storage rather than duplicated.
- Medical infection risk is split across wound care, sanitation, and medicine caution.
- Movement logic is split between deciding to move and how to move safely.
- Digital resilience is split between communications, knowledge preservation, and dystopian fallback systems.
- Household stability is split across preparedness routines, leadership, and work rotation.

Do not add near-duplicate future files with names such as:
- basic_survival_tips.json
- emergency_general_advice.json
- first_aid_basics_2.json
- water_basics_extra.json
Instead expand the relevant existing files.
