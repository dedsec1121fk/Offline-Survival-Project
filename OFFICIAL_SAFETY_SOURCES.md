# Official safety sources used for future verification

This project is offline-first, but safety guidance should stay aligned with reliable public-health and emergency-preparedness sources when possible.

Useful official/public sources to check during future updates:

- CDC — How to Create and Store an Emergency Water Supply  
  https://www.cdc.gov/water-emergency/about/how-to-create-and-store-an-emergency-water-supply.html

- CDC — How to Make Water Safe in an Emergency  
  https://www.cdc.gov/water-emergency/about/index.html

- CDC — How to Find Clean Water in an Emergency  
  https://www.cdc.gov/water-emergency/about/how-to-find-clean-water-in-an-emergency.html

- CDC — Keep Food Safe After a Disaster or Emergency  
  https://www.cdc.gov/food-safety/foods/keep-food-safe-after-emergency.html

- CDC — What to Do to Protect Yourself During a Power Outage  
  https://www.cdc.gov/natural-disasters/response/what-to-do-protect-yourself-during-a-power-outage.html

- American Red Cross — Power Outage Safety  
  https://www.redcross.org/get-help/how-to-prepare-for-emergencies/types-of-emergencies/power-outage.html

- American Red Cross — Food Safety During an Emergency  
  https://www.redcross.org/get-help/how-to-prepare-for-emergencies/types-of-emergencies/food-safety.html

Maintenance rule:
- Keep exact medical dosing, chemical mixing, diagnosis, electrical repair, gas repair, structural repair, and hazardous cleanup advice out of the database unless copied from a current official source and clearly framed as official guidance.
- Prefer safe boundaries, records, triage, warning signs, and instructions to contact qualified help.
